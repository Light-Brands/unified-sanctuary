Where Land and People Heal Together

Unified Sanctuaries is a regenerative, land-based community committed to solving for each other's basic needs of food, housing, and community within a supportive, earth-friendly environment.

Like mycelium beneath a forest floor, we're creating four interconnected platforms, each distinct yet fundamentally inseparable, sharing resources, wisdom, and life force through invisible threads of mutual support.

"Land heals people. People heal land. This is the spiral we're tending."

The Four Platforms

Four interconnected pillars, one living ecosystem

01

Permaculture Farm & Education

Regenerative agriculture, food sovereignty, and hands-on learning. From soil to table to community.

02

Retreat & Healing Arts

Immersive wellness, somatic healing, bathhouse sanctuary. A place to return to wholeness.

03

Event Venue

Festivals, gatherings, markets, and celebrations. Where community comes alive.

04

Community Living

20+ homes woven together. Shared resources, earned equity, a place in the web.

Rooted in Values

The principles that guide everything we grow and every decision we make together.

Regeneration

We actively heal rather than merely sustain. Every action should leave the land and community better.

Non-Speculative Stewardship

Land is held in perpetual trust, removed from market speculation forever.

Equity Through Contribution

Members earn ownership through participation, creating true skin in the game.

Participatory Governance

Decisions flow from circles of consent, not hierarchy or majority rule.

Open-Source Sharing

Our model becomes a blueprint others can adapt and replicate.

Indigenous Wisdom Honored

We acknowledge Abenaki stewardship and commit to reciprocity.

The Unified Sanctuaries Approach

Rooted in Southern Vermont

We're searching for our forever home in the Brattleboro, Guilford, and Putney region, rich rolling hills, river valleys, and a community already oriented toward sustainability and creativity.

Vermont's agricultural heritage, progressive policies, and existing networks of land trusts and cooperatives make it the ideal soil for this experiment.
