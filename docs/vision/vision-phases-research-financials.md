

# **Unified Sanctuaries Vision** 

# **Short Description**

In the heart of nature, Unified Sanctuaries envisions an innovative tapestry—a collection of distinct, interconnected entities harmoniously coexisting on separate, adjacent plots of land. This concept comprises a permaculture farm, a vibrant community, an event venue available for rent, and a transformative retreat and healing arts center, including a makerspace. Each plot stands alone yet collaborates seamlessly, embodying the essence of regeneration and accessibility.

## **The Farm:**

One of the cornerstones of this vision is the farm—a lush expanse where the earth is cultivated with care. Complete with fertile soil, orchards of fruit trees, fields of companion-planted veggies, pollinators, and hemp, barns, a gourmet mushroom grow, storage facilities, a compost operation, and a farm stand, it will symbolize our commitment to nurturing the land, with permaculture principles and regenerative agriculture guiding our process. A portion of the harvest will be allocated to feed the local home free population, embodying the project’s ethos of giving back to the community.

## **Community Living Space:**

Adjacent to the farm, a vibrant community of kindred spirits will reside, each of the 15+ households stewarding its own 1-2 acres of land. This environment will foster individuality within a supportive community framework. A central communal structure will serve as the hub for shared activities and basic needs to be met, i.e. cooking, bathing, bathrooms, laundry, etc.. These community members will play pivotal roles in the adjacent properties and businesses \- as partners, advisors, and / or operators.

## **Event Space for Rent:**

Unified Sanctuaries will offer an event space, indoor and outdoor, available for rent by aligned organizations, groups, entrepreneurs, artists, educators, and practitioners alike. Accessible through a thoughtful application process, this venue will serve as an affordable platform for local people and small businesses \- encouraging innovation, creativity, and meaningful collaborations within the community.

## **Retreat, Healing Arts, & Education Center:**

Adjacent to the farm and the community living space, the Retreat and Healing Arts Center will take shape—a sanctuary of transformation. Healing workshops, visual and performing arts events, a makerspace, and a variety of therapeutic modalities will find their place. Spa services (offered inside a bathhouse), art and nature therapy, and creative expression will be facilitated. A portion of the center will feature untouched land—a haven for exploration, camping, and self-expression, including areas where individuals are free to be naked, providing a legal and liberating environment for self-expression. Additionally, a percentage of every ticket sold, booking, and purchase made on site will go towards a scholarship fund to cover the cost of services, workshops, and events for those who cannot afford them. Social justice, equity, and access to healing, education, and pleasure will be interwoven into all programs, promoting understanding, compassion, and thrive-ability among our diverse guests.

## **The Vision of Regeneration and Accessibility:**

The essence of this vision lies in the profound "why:” a collective desire to regenerate ourselves, our communities, our bioregion, our Earth; and to cultivate deeper connection in all of these relationships. 

Unified Sanctuaries is a living testament to accessible knowledge, showcasing the potential of learning-by-doing and the power of “many hands making light work.” We plan to begin with a phased approach to development and to grow into a thriving revenue-generating retreat center, farm, space for rent for events, and community of residents, artists, stewards, entrepreneurs, wellness practitioners, scientists, and more.

This vision is an invitation to embrace the boundless possibilities of collaboration, community, and regeneration. The overarching intention is for Unified Sanctuaries to embody love, inclusivity, accessibility, learning, healing, and natural harmony; to foster a deep connection between individuals, communities, and the natural world.

## **Nature Therapy Data US 2023**

Recent research studies have illuminated the significant impact of nature exposure on human well-being. Spending a minimum of 120 minutes per week in natural environments substantially improves overall health and mental well-being, as evidenced by a [study](https://www.uc.edu/news/articles/2023/08/the-healing-power-of-nature.html) conducted in 2019 with 20,000 participants. The World Health Organization's report further emphasizes the positive effects of spending time in nature, even in urban areas, including improved moods, mental health, and mindset ([Source](https://www.uc.edu/news/articles/2023/08/the-healing-power-of-nature.html)).

Nature exposure addresses stress-related issues, crucial in a context where eight out of ten Americans experience stress at least once every two weeks. The rise in healthcare spending in the United States, reaching 20% of the GDP, has necessitated alternative approaches, making nature therapy a viable solution. Excessive screen time, averaging over 10 hours a day, has contributed to the growing divide between humans and nature, leading to various health problems ([Source](https://www.uc.edu/news/articles/2023/08/the-healing-power-of-nature.html)).

Urbanization, with over 80% of Americans residing in urban areas, has further disconnected individuals from the natural world. Accessible green spaces have demonstrated remarkable positive outcomes, reducing obesity-related hospital admissions and heart-related issues ([Source](https://today.tamu.edu/2023/10/23/connection-with-nature-linked-to-longer-healthier-lives/)). Hospitals with nature views significantly expedited post-operation recovery, indicating the healing effect of natural surroundings.

Cognitive and emotional benefits of nature exposure are extensive, fostering enhanced cognitive development in children and improved attentional functioning in adults ([Source](https://www.apa.org/monitor/2020/04/nurtured-nature)). Nature exposure promotes emotional well-being, encouraging happiness, positive social interactions, and a sense of purpose in life. As part of the United Nations' sustainable development goals, providing universal access to safe, inclusive, and accessible green spaces by 2030 is recognized as a pivotal policy implication.

Furthermore, the practice of Forest-Bathing, or Shinrin-Yoku, originating in Japan, involves slow walks in nature, engaging the senses to absorb the natural world's benefits ([Source](https://www.wvia.org/news/local/2023-03-13/the-healing-power-of-nature-the-rise-of-forest-bathing)). Scientifically proven, phytoncides emitted by trees decrease stress hormones, improve sleep, and positively impact mood. Guided forest therapy walks facilitate a deeper connection with nature, promoting overall well-being and offering an effective therapeutic modality.

## **Art Therapy Data US 2023**

Art therapy emerges as a transformative and versatile intervention across various studies, demonstrating profound benefits for mental health. A randomized trial in 2022 revealed its efficacy in alleviating depression and anxiety symptoms among individuals with stable major depressive disorder, emphasizing its therapeutic potential ([Source](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9472646/#:~:text=This%20randomized%2C%20single%2Dblind%20trial,improved%20depression%20and%20anxiety%20symptoms)).

In a broader context, a 2021 study emphasized the pivotal role of art therapy in conditions such as cancer, depression, anxiety, autism, and dementia. Acting as a conduit for expression when verbal communication becomes challenging, art therapy positively influences mental disorders, providing an effective and complementary therapeutic method ([Source](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8397377/)).

The advantages of art therapy span a spectrum, ranging from pain alleviation and stress reduction to enhanced quality of life for cancer patients. It stimulates mental function in older adults with dementia, reduces depression in Parkinson's patients, and has demonstrated effectiveness in managing symptoms associated with various health conditions ([Source](https://www.canr.msu.edu/news/the_benefits_art_therapy_can_have_on_mental_and_physical_health)).

Furthermore, the World Health Organization's synthesis report in 2022 underscored the vital role of art therapies, including visual arts, music therapy, dance therapy, and drama therapy, in promoting health and managing cognitive and psychological disorders. Art therapy methods were acknowledged as valuable complementary treatments for conditions such as depression, stress, anxiety, and even the effects of chronic diseases ([Source](https://www.intechopen.com/chapters/82189)).

Art therapy extends its impact beyond mental health, with studies revealing reduced cortisol levels, alleviation of stress and anxiety, and significant benefits for individuals dealing with trauma, depression, or anxiety. This holistic therapeutic approach offers promise in diverse scenarios, emphasizing its potential as a powerful tool for mental health and overall well-being ([Source](https://www.forbes.com/health/mind/art-therapy/)). These findings collectively highlight art therapy's multifaceted contributions, making it a compelling inclusion in our retreat and healing arts center's offerings.

## **Psychedelic Therapy Data US 2023**

The Multidisciplinary Association for Psychedelic Studies (MAPS) completed its second Phase 3 trial on MDMA for PTSD, projecting FDA evaluation in 2023 and holding a breakthrough designation. This resurgence of interest in psychedelics extends beyond the laboratory, reflecting a changing legal landscape.
